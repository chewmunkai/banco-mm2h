Children & schooling

Your children, settled and thriving.

Malaysia is one of Asia's most established homes for international education — and your children can enrol on a dependent or student pass arranged right alongside your MM2H application.

  • Curricula on your doorstep — British (IGCSE & A-Levels), International Baccalaureate, American, Australian and Canadian programmes across Kuala Lumpur, Penang and Iskandar Puteri.
  • From day schools to British boarding names — we shortlist by curriculum, commute and budget, then arrange the visits.
  • Passes for every child — dependent and student passes lodged with your application and renewed in step with your stay.
  • A clear path to university — Malaysia hosts branch campuses of UK and Australian universities, with onward routes to the UK, US and beyond.

We handle placement, registration and the paperwork; you choose the school that feels right.

Property & ownership

A home to buy, or a place to lease.

Foreigners can own property in Malaysia in their own name — most homes are freehold or long leasehold. We make the search, the checks and the financing feel effortless.

  • Own above a state minimum — each state sets a minimum purchase price (commonly around RM 1,000,000, with lower thresholds in some states and zones). We confirm what applies to you.
  • Buy or lease — curated condominiums and landed homes in KL (Mont Kiara, Bangsar, KLCC, Desa ParkCity), Penang and Iskandar Puteri — with rentals while you settle.
  • Verified before you sign — independent title checks and a vetted conveyancing lawyer on every purchase.
  • Financing for non-residents — through our banking partners, with clear guidance on the categories foreigners cannot buy.

Property and visa rules are set by the government and revised from time to time — we verify the current thresholds before you commit.

Good to know

MM2H, answered plainly

The honest version — what the programme is, what it lets you do, and where the official rules live.

Who is MM2H for?

Malaysia My Second Home is a long-stay visa for foreigners who want to live in Malaysia without giving up their citizenship — retirees, families, remote professionals and investors. It is renewable, and your spouse, children and, in many cases, parents can join you as dependents.

Can my children study in Malaysia?

Yes. Children join on a dependent or student pass and attend Malaysia's international and private schools — British, IB, American, Australian and more. We arrange placement and the pass paperwork. See Children & schooling above.

Can I buy a home — and what can foreigners own?

Foreigners can own property in their own name above a minimum purchase price set by each state (commonly around RM 1,000,000, with variations). Some categories — such as Malay-reserved land and certain low-cost units — are not open to foreign buyers. You can also lease while you settle. See Property & ownership.

Can I work or run a business on MM2H?

MM2H is a residency visa, not an automatic work permit. Employment, part-time work and running a business each have their own rules and passes, and these change from time to time. We advise on the right structure for your plans.

What are the financial requirements?

The government sets fixed-deposit, offshore-income and property thresholds. They are revised periodically and differ by tier and by state — Sarawak and Sabah run their own programmes. Rather than quote a figure that may already be out of date, we confirm the current requirements for your exact situation and point you to the official sources.

How is healthcare in Malaysia?

Malaysia's private hospitals are excellent and far more affordable than in the West, and many are internationally accredited. We recommend private health cover and can introduce trusted providers.

How are taxes handled?

Malaysia generally taxes income sourced in Malaysia, and the treatment of foreign-sourced income has its own rules that have changed in recent years. We connect you with vetted tax advisors so your situation is structured correctly — we don't make one-size-fits-all tax promises.

How long does approval take?

Timelines vary with the programme and your paperwork and are typically measured in months. We prepare and lodge everything, then track each milestone so you always know where things stand.

What does Banco actually do?

Everything, in one place: eligibility, documents, lodging your application, property, schooling, banking, tax introductions and settling-in support — one dedicated advisor from first enquiry to arrivals.

Official requirements rest with the Government of Malaysia. For the current programme rules, see the official MM2H portal and the Immigration Department of Malaysia — we'll confirm everything that applies to your situation.
